About TrueMath

TrueMath is the auditable math engine for AI-age professionals. AI handles the conversation; TrueMath handles the math. Every answer is deterministic, traceable to its inputs, and ready to share. For M&A advisors, that means valuation, returns, and tax math your client and counterparty can both stake their reputations on.

What you calculate

From the back of the napkin to the closing waterfall.

The M&A Advisor domain spans the full lifecycle of a deal: valuing the target, structuring the deal, modeling the returns, and walking the seller through what they actually take home.

Earnings & adjustments
Build EBITDA and SDE from financials. Apply add-backs to get to defensible adjusted earnings. Track margins and multi-year trends.
  • EBITDA / SDE build-up
  • Adjusted EBITDA, adjusted SDE
  • Margin analysis, growth rates
  • Trend tables and charts
Valuation
EBITDA, SDE, and revenue multiples. DCF with direct cash flows or driver-based projections. Low-mid-high scenario ranges.
  • Multiple-based EV
  • DCF with WACC and terminal value
  • Driver-based DCF projection
  • Valuation range scenarios
Equity value bridge
Move from enterprise value to equity value the way the term sheet says: net debt, working capital adjustment against the negotiated peg.
  • Net debt
  • Working capital peg and surplus
  • EV ↔ equity value
LBO returns
Size the debt, model the equity check, project the exit. MOIC, IRR, payback, year-one cash-on-cash, full sensitivity grid.
  • Sources and uses
  • Debt sizing and DSCR
  • Buyer equity, MOIC, IRR
  • Entry × exit sensitivity grid
Seller proceeds & tax
Purchase price allocation across tangibles, intangibles, non-compete, and goodwill. Three tax rates flow into the seven-row seller waterfall.
  • Advisory fee with floor
  • Purchase price allocation
  • Recapture, ordinary, capital gains
  • Net cash to seller waterfall
Earnouts, synergies & A/D
Earnout PV, probability-weighted deal value, capitalized synergy value, strategic price ceilings, and acquirer accretion or dilution.
  • Earnout PV and schedule
  • Probability-weighted deal value
  • Synergy value and price ceiling
  • Pro forma EPS, accretion / dilution
