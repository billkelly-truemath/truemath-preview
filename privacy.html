How TrueMath Processes Your Data

Before describing the categories of personal information we collect, it helps to understand how TrueMath actually processes data, because the architecture matters for privacy.

The Services consist of two layers. The first is a deterministic mathematical computation engine that runs on TrueMath's infrastructure inside Amazon Web Services. The second is a natural language interpretation layer powered by Anthropic's Claude, accessed through Amazon Bedrock. When you submit a request, the interpretation layer converts your natural language into a structured calculation request, and the computation engine executes the calculation.

This split is important because:

  • Your inputs and the prompts derived from them pass through Amazon Bedrock to Anthropic's Claude
  • Under AWS's published Bedrock terms, your prompts and completions are not used to train any foundation model, model providers (including Anthropic) do not have access to them, and the data stays inside AWS infrastructure during the call
  • TrueMath does not train any large language model on your inputs, your outputs, or any content you submit to the Services
  • The deterministic computation engine processes your inputs entirely within TrueMath's AWS environment

The current Bedrock data privacy commitments are published at aws.amazon.com/bedrock/security-compliance.
